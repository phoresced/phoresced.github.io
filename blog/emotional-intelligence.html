Training Emotional Intelligence: An exercise in core skills.


What is Emotional Intelligence?

EQ stands for Emotional 'Quotient', which measures our ability to notice, regulate and express the emotions of ourselves and others.

How is it different to IQ?

Basically, IQ focuses on the head while EQ observes the heart.
IQ measures rationality, cognitive capacity, and rote skills like analytical ability or reasoning. EQ measures our ability to create harmony in the way we feel, process, do things and interact with others.

Source: 6seconds.org

As demonstrated in the graph above, EQ is a better measure of 'success' if success is defined as Effectiveness, Relationships, Wellbeing & Quality of Life. Notice the trend in this randomized and global sample of over 75,000 individuals.

Why is it important?

EQ gives us greater ability to process stress, conflict, and other unsavoury human experiences. It is about cultivating wisdom to act in ways which are healthy for us and those we interact with, rather than worsening a problem, self-destructing or causing harm.

Since we need to interact with a lot of people in various parts of life (including the workplace), it's important to participate in cultivating a healthy and supportive social environment.

In work, this means bringing out the best in each other.
